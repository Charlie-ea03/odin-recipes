Carbonara Recipe

Homepage

Description

This classic Italian dish brings a nostalgic yet proffessional flavour to you dinner table. And it's so easy to cook, all it takes is 5 minutes prep time, and 10 minutes cooking time. Making this dish perfect for when you are in a rush, home late from work, or just fancy a quick, low effort dish.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti
  • Eggs (Yolk)
  • Guanciale or Pancetta
  • Pecorino Romano Cheese
  • Parmasan Cheese
  • Black Pepper
  • Olive Oil

Instructions

  1. Cut the Guanciale/Pancetta into 1cm^2 cubes.
  2. Put the Guanciale on low heat in a frying pan to render
  3. In a saucepan, fill half way (depending on how much pasta you are using) with water, with a tbls of salt.
  4. Seperate the yolk from the egg whites and keep the yolks in a bowl. You can discard the egg whites.
  5. Mix the Pecorino and Parmasan cheese in with the yolks until the mixture starts to firm.
  6. Add a few pinches of ground black pepper to the mixture (depending on your preference).
  7. Add the spaghetti to the now boiling water.
  8. Drain the pasta, making sure to save a cup of the water to one side.
  9. Pour a small amount of olive oil over the pasta to prevent them sticking together.
  10. Pour the egg yolk mixture into the pan with the now rendered Guanciale.
  11. Almost straight after adding the mixture to the pan, add the spaghetti and stir.
  12. Add the pasta water until the sauce is viscous enough to smother the spaghetti (ensure that they are not drowned and that there is minimal excess liquid in the pan).
  13. Once the sauce is smooth and seems creamy, remove your pasta from the pan and serve with a sprinkle of parmasan and ground black pepper.
  14. Sit down, relax, and enjoy your easy, traditional Carbonara.
